# Expert Validation — Protocol

> **Purpose**: calibrate the simulated focus-group method against real professional knowledge.
>
> This document describes how MirrorBuddy's expert validation programme works: who the experts are, how sessions are run, and how findings from simulated runs are cross-checked. Expert input does not replace user research with real children; it fills the calibration gap between AI-simulated persona sessions and live user studies.

---

## 1. Background and motivation

The MirrorBuddy simulated focus-group method uses AI agents role-playing synthetic DSA student personas (see `docs/focus-group/runs/` for executed runs). Every run carries a mandatory caveat:

> _"Metodo NON calibrato su utenti reali. Ogni dato qui è prodotto da agenti AI che interpretano studenti DSA, non da bambini reali."_

Expert validation is the first calibration layer before live user studies. Qualified professionals review:

1. Whether the **synthetic persona descriptions** (dyslexia, ADHD, autism, motor, sensory profiles) are clinically plausible.
2. Whether the **findings and usability claims** from simulated runs match real-world professional experience with similar populations.
3. Whether the **severity rankings** (S1–S3) and priority proposals reflect appropriate clinical weighting.

Expert input generates **calibration deltas**: findings the experts consider over-stated, under-stated, or missing entirely from the simulated corpus.

## 3. Materials provided to each expert

For each validation session the expert receives:

1. **Platform brief** (1 page): what MirrorBuddy is, target users, AI character system, current UI surface being evaluated.
2. **Filtered finding table**: the subset of findings from the simulated runs relevant to their specialty (e.g., E01 receives findings about text complexity, readability, vocabulary).
3. **Representative quotes** (clearly labelled `[SIMULATO]`): 3–5 verbatim quotes per finding from the simulated personas, translated to Italian where necessary.
4. **Their questionnaire** (this repository, `questionnaires/`): structured questions in the expert's domain.
5. **UI screenshots** (PNG artefacts from `stimulus/` packs) for findings that have pixel-verified evidence.

Experts are explicitly told:

- All quotes are generated by AI agents, not real children.
- Their role is to assess plausibility and completeness, not to validate the AI method itself.
- They may add findings not present in the simulated corpus.

---

## 4. Session format

Sessions are conducted remotely (video call + screen share, 60–90 min) or asynchronously (questionnaire return within 7 days). The facilitator (a MirrorBuddy team member) walks through each questionnaire section and records free-text annotations alongside the expert's responses.

### 4.1 Session phases

| Phase          | Duration  | Content                                                                                          |
| -------------- | --------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Brief          | 10 min    | Platform overview, method disclosure, consent                                                    |
| Persona review | 15 min    | Expert reviews synthetic persona card for their specialty area                                   |
| Finding review | 30–45 min | Questionnaire: rating each finding's clinical plausibility, severity agreement, and completeness |
| Open additions | 10–15 min | Expert proposes findings not present in simulated corpus                                         |
| Debrief        | 5 min     | Questions, next steps                                                                            |

### 4.2 Rating scales

All finding-level ratings use the same scales across all questionnaires:

**Clinical plausibility** (1–4):

- 1 = Implausible / would not occur with this profile
- 2 = Possible but unlikely
- 3 = Plausible and consistent with clinical experience
- 4 = Highly plausible / commonly observed

**Severity agreement** (S1 / S2 / S3 / lower / higher):

- Expert either confirms the simulated severity or proposes an adjustment.
- S1 = blocks primary task; S2 = significant friction; S3 = minor annoyance.

**Priority confirmation** (yes / no / conditional):

- Whether the proposed dev/design action is clinically appropriate as stated.

---

## 5. Output and calibration delta

After each expert session a **calibration delta document** is produced (one per expert, stored alongside their questionnaire output):

```
docs/focus-group/expert-validation/deltas/
  E01-logopedist-delta.md
  E02-special-education-teacher-delta.md
  …
```

Each delta records:

- **Confirmed findings**: simulated findings the expert rates ≥ 3 (plausible) and agrees with the severity.
- **Downgraded findings**: ratings ≤ 2 or severity lower — these lose one confidence tier in the master finding table.
- **Upgraded findings**: severity raised by expert — these gain a `⬆ expert-upgraded` tag in the master table.
- **New findings**: findings proposed by the expert not present in the simulated corpus — these enter the master table as `[EXPERT]` class findings, confidence ❓ until cross-checked against real user studies.

The master finding table (`docs/focus-group/master-findings.md`, created after the first full validation round) aggregates confirmed, adjusted, and expert-added findings across all validators.

---

## 6. Limitations and ethics

- Expert validation **reduces but does not eliminate** the calibration gap. It replaces one type of indirect evidence (AI simulation) with another (professional opinion). Only live sessions with real children remove the gap fully.
- Experts review **simulated data**. They are not evaluating real children's experiences.
- Expert sessions follow GDPR Article 6(1)(b) (contractual/professional service). No sensitive personal data is collected. Experts' names and affiliations are stored internally but not published in repository documents (use E01–E05 codes externally).
- Experts are compensated for their time. Compensation does not affect the objectivity requirement; the questionnaires include explicit plausibility ratings to surface disagreement.
- **COPPA**: no children participate in expert validation sessions.

---

## 7. Relationship to simulated focus-group method

```
Simulated run (AI personas)
        ↓
  Findings + quotes [SIMULATO]
        ↓
  Expert validation (this protocol)
        ↓
  Calibration delta → upgraded master finding table
        ↓
  Live user studies (real children, future phase)
        ↓
  Final validated finding set
```

Expert validation outputs feed back into the simulated method:

- Implausible persona behaviours → revise synthetic persona cards.
- Missing findings → add to future simulated run canary/probe bank.
- Severity disagreements → update the severity rubric in the method spec.
